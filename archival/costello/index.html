Csound Compositions & Instruments

The following compositions & instruments were created by Sean Costello from November of 1998 to October of 1999, except for monkeystretch which is new. All compositions copyright © 1999 by Sean Costello, except monkeystretch which is copyrighted for 2000. All rights reserved.

stringphaser - A recreation of the classic "string ensemble" through a phaser that was heavily used in 1970's electronic music, with a Feedback Delay Network reverb.

choir - A choir simulation that predates the string ensemble simulation, but is based on a similar concept. Uses resonz filters to generate formants.

beethoven - Beethoven's 9th, 2nd movement, subjected to various processing: frequency shifting (based on my hilbert ugen), "barberpole" phasing (using hilbert and allpassn), and my implementation of the Stautner/Puckette FDN reverb.

line, surface, space - Three seperate small compositions, based around the concepts of line, surface, and space (or my interpretations thereof). All three of the compositions use a single sample - a 1.3 second snippet of William S. Burroughs saying the word "Apocalypse" - as the sole sound source. Various combinations of filtering, echo, reverberation, and granular synthesis are used to twist the sample beyond recognition.

otis - My first proper Csound composition. Uses simple granular synthesis, additive synthesis of noise-modulated sinusoids, and frequency modulation synthesis. Common Music was used in the generation of the score file - it is enormous (88 KB).

pvguitar - A steel-string guitar fingerpicking passage, transformed through phase vocoder analysis/resynthesis (using pvadd) into a sound that is vaguely kalimba-esque.

monkeystretch - Timestretched monkeys. Siamang, even. (Note: this is a pretty big .wav file - about 10 megs or so.)

gong - A gong sound, using delay lines in a feedback delay network, with passive nonlinear filters in the loops (a la Van Duyne) to create a buildup of harmonic energy. .orc .sco

intro - The beginning of a composition, in search of the rest of the composition. The first thing I ever did in Csound that sounded good.

SynthBuilder Stuff

The following "pieces" were done at odd moments in my job at Staccato Systems, usually while trying to do something else. Copyright © 2000 by Sean Costello, all rights reserved, and all that.

ghostElectrons - While exploring a particular method of making a time-varying reverb, I came across the following sound. I am running white noise into the reverb, and tweaking the controls in real time.

ggggggg - Done late at night, after working all Saturday. This throws in the kitchen sink: a digital sub-octave cross-product generator (modeled after an Electronotes circuit), running into a MoogVCF unit generator, through a frequency shifter set up for barberpole phasing, into a ring modulator that is being LFOed. Sample rate was set wrong, which accounts for some strange resonances which I like. Again, just tweaking controls in real time. I really need to figure out better names for my pieces.
